What do you really want to do before you die? It’s a tough question, and one that’s even tougher to contemplate. You’re 40 in a few months, which is wild considering you never thought you’d make it to 30. Now you talk about being lucky to make 70… maybe you need to ask yourself why you didn’t think you’d make it to 30, as well as what you’d do if you made it to 80 and have another 40 years of life ahead of you.

But the question is what you want to do before you die, and not if you’ll live to 80. You want to shed all the excess, all the attachments you have - to be a completed book without loose ends or appendices. You have books to write, and it’s nice to acknowledge that you’d spend your last days writing if you could. It’s not about being remembered, it’s about creating something you put your whole soul into, something that utterly consumes you, that pours out of you like water, that merges with you as a person where the book is as much you as you are it.

Setting an arbitrary number of books to write before you die is meaningless, especially if you just use it as a timeline to measure and wipe yourself with. Writing articles for magazines and journals means less too - it’s like you’re doing 5-10 minute comedy sets again. It doesn’t mean you can’t or won’t, but the real fulfilment comes from writing full length books that you are truly proud of in the end.

You have so many adventures behind you that feel like emotional boat anchors, dragging along the ocean floor and tripping you up as you look at them and think “coulda, woulda, shoulda”. Well you have absolutely nothing to lose - write. Don’t worry about if you’re writing the right thing or not, just develop the things that you know are calling to you now. Don’t be afraid to start, fuck up, change course, and start again - none of this is wasted, and returning every day to write a mini essay is exactly the kind of writing muscle you need to develop so you can write thousands of words a day again as you focus on a book.

So don’t worry about trying to tick things off a bucket list before you die for the sake of it - figure out what you’re going to do today, how to use the winds of circumstance to push you towards who you want to be every day, and then get up tomorrow to be a little bit better at being that person tomorrow. This is the way of the peaceful warrior, and you’ll never “achieve” it - you can only walk it each day.
